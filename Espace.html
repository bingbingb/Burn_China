Artificial Earth Satellite

  On April 24, 1970, China successfully developed and launched the first man-made earth satellite, Dongfanghong-1. It has become the fifth country in the world to independently develop and launch artificial earth satellites. At present, China has made corresponding achievements in four satellite series, such as the returnable remote sensing satellite series. "Dongfanghong" communication and broadcasting satellite series, "Fengyun" meteorological satellite series and "Practice" scientific exploration and technical test satellite series.

  The Long March series launch vehicle is a space vehicle developed by China itself. The Long March launch vehicle started in the 1960s. On April 24, 1970, the first launch of the Dongfanghong-1 satellite by the Long March-1 launch vehicle was successful. As of 13 January 2018, China's Long March series launch vehicles have flown 263 times, with a success rate of 94.297%.

Spacecraft Launch Center Site

  China has built four spacecraft launch sites, Jiuquan, Xichang, Taiyuan and Hainan, and successfully completed them. Flight tests of various launch vehicles and launching missions of various artificial satellites and test spacecraft. China's spacecraft launch site can accomplish domestic launching tasks. It also has the ability to complete services for international commercial launches and other international space cooperation.
