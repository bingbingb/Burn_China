Port History

  China's waterway development has a long history, ranging from the Neolithic Age to the feudal dynasty to the founding of New China. China's ports have their own historical context.
  As early as the Neolithic Age, canoes and rafts were widely used in natural rivers. The wooden oars unearthed from Hemudu, Zhejiang Province, prove that fishermen along the southeastern coast of China used oars to go fishing and hunting more than 2,000 years ago.
